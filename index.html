Galactic Government Declares Emergency Summit After Mars–Neptune Resource Treaty Breakdown

By Astra Velorum • Feb 1, 2108

Tensions in the inner solar system reached a critical point this morning as the long-standing Mars–Neptune Resource Treaty officially collapsed. The Galactic Government announced an emergency summit to prevent what experts fear could become the first ever interplanetary political crisis for the galaxy. Early reports suggest that disputes over helium-3 mining rights in the asteroid belt triggered the sudden dissolution of the agreement, leaving both colonies scrambling for diplomatic footing…

According to Council representatives, the talks will focus on stabilizing trade routes through the Neptunian system and renegotiating energy distribution protocols that have governed interplanetary cooperation since 2100. Political analysts warn that if no resolution is reached, the split could threaten fuel supplies, disrupt quantum communication corridors, and destabilize smaller colonies that depend on Neptunian resources.

Meanwhile, Mars officials publicly accused Neptune’s orbital coalition of violating Uranus's shared mining borders, a claim the Neptunian diplomats strongly deny. The government's emergency session—set to begin within the next 48 hours—may determine the direction of solar-system governance for decades to come.
